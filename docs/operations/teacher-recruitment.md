# Teacher Recruitment Strategy
## Ballet Academy and Movement

> **Goal:** Identify and recruit talented ballet instructors who align with BAM's culture, elevate the program, and grow with the studio long-term.

---

## 1. Recruitment Philosophy

BAM does not just hire instructors — it builds a teaching faculty.

The right teacher for BAM:
- Has genuine classical ballet training (not just performance experience)
- Loves working with children ages 3-12
- Aligns with a nurturing, student-first teaching philosophy
- Has a reputation that brings students (or will build one at BAM)
- Sees teaching as a craft, not just a job

**What we're not hiring:**
- Generic "dance teacher" without classical foundation
- Competition-factory instructors who prioritize trophies over development
- Teachers looking for a temporary gig

---

## 2. Ideal Candidate Profiles

### Profile A — The Experienced Studio Teacher
- 3+ years teaching at another studio
- Has a loyal student following
- Dissatisfied with their current environment (comp factory, management issues, culture mismatch)
- Wants to teach in a more respected, nurturing setting

**Recruitment signal:** Talking about leaving, mentions burnout, has strong reputation in the community

---

### Profile B — The Former Performer Transitioning to Teaching
- Professional or semi-professional ballet career winding down
- Interested in passing on knowledge
- Strong technique foundation
- May need support on pedagogy and classroom management (onboarding covers this)

**Recruitment signal:** Announcing end of performing career, posting about next chapter

---

### Profile C — The Strong Part-Time Instructor
- College student in dance program or recent graduate
- Strong technique, enthusiastic about teaching
- Wants real experience beyond assisting
- Right fit for younger classes (Tiny Dancers, Pre-Ballet)

**Recruitment signal:** Looking for part-time teaching work, posting about teaching interest

## 3. Where to Find Candidates

### Active Sourcing

| Source | Method |
|--------|--------|
| Competing studios | Identify teachers by name from studio websites + social accounts |
| Regional dance programs | Cal State Long Beach, UCI, Chapman — watch graduating seniors |
| Local performing arts organizations | Segerstrom, Pacific Symphony ballet performances |
| Summer intensives | Teachers who attend or work at regional summer programs |
| YAGP (Youth America Grand Prix) | Coaches and teachers visible at competitions |
| Instagram | Search `#balletteacher` + location tags for South OC |
| LinkedIn | Search: "ballet instructor" + Orange County |

### Passive Sourcing (Always On)

- BAM Instagram: post content that makes the studio look like a great place to teach
- Indeed job posting (currently live): Office Manager and Ballet Instructor
- Word of mouth: Amanda's professional network + past colleagues
- Parent referrals: "Do you know any talented teachers looking for a studio?"

## 4. Competitive Studios to Monitor for Talent

Watch these studios for teachers who may be open to a move:

| Studio | Why They Might Leave |
|--------|---------------------|
| San Clemente Dance Academy | Comp-heavy culture may not suit classical-focused instructors |
| Southland Ballet | Geographic opportunity — closer studio for some teachers |
| Capistrano Academy | Culture fit issues possible |
| South Coast Conservatory | Budget constraints at higher-end studios |
| Any studio with management/ownership changes | Instability = openness to move |

**Monitoring method:**
- Follow teacher Instagram accounts (via studio account)
- Note if they stop tagging their studio or begin posting more personally
- Watch for "exciting news coming soon" signals

## 6. Interview Process

**Stage 1 — Phone or video call (20-30 min)**
- Teaching background and philosophy
- Experience with ages 3-12
- What they're looking for in a studio environment
- Why they're open to a change

**Stage 2 — Observe a class at BAM**
- Candidate watches Amanda teach for 30-45 minutes
- Get a feel for the culture, pace, studio energy
- Informal conversation afterward

**Stage 3 — Trial teach (30-45 min with students)**
- Candidate leads a portion of class
- Amanda observes
- Evaluate: rapport with children, technique corrections, classroom energy

**Stage 4 — Offer conversation**
- Pay structure, schedule, expectations
- Start date

## 7. Retention — Keeping Good Teachers

The best recruiting strategy is never needing to recruit:

| Initiative | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Clear pay categories (M4) | Teachers know exactly what they earn per session type |
| Professional development | Amanda mentors new teachers; send to workshops and masterclasses |
| Culture investment | Teachers feel valued, not interchangeable |
| Performance involvement | Teachers share in the pride of productions and student achievements |
| Badge system | Teachers are the ones who award skill badges — meaningful role |
| Recognition | Celebrate teacher milestones publicly (social, in-studio) |
