# Mandated Reporter Protocol
## Ballet Academy and Movement

> **This document is required reading for all teachers and staff before their first class.**  
> **California law requires all dance instructors to complete AB 1432 training. Certificates must be on file.**

---

## 1. Who Is a Mandated Reporter?

Under California law (Penal Code Section 11165.7), dance instructors and employees of dance studios are mandated reporters.

**Every teacher and staff member at Ballet Academy and Movement is a mandated reporter.**

This includes:
- All lead instructors
- All assistant instructors
- All sub teachers
- Administrative staff with student contact
- Anyone observing classes as part of their role

---

## 2. What Must Be Reported

You are required to report **reasonable suspicion** of:

| Category | Examples |
|---------|---------|
| Physical abuse | Unexplained bruises, burns, cuts; injuries inconsistent with explanation |
| Sexual abuse | Inappropriate touching, sexualized behavior in a child, disclosure from child |
| Emotional abuse | Severe humiliation, threats, terrorizing by caregiver |
| Severe neglect | Consistent hunger, inappropriate clothing for weather, signs of medical neglect |
| Self-harm risk | Cutting, burning, student discloses they are hurting themselves |
| Bullying (safety level) | Systematic targeting that creates reasonable fear for a student's safety |

### What "Reasonable Suspicion" Means

You do **not** need proof. You do not need certainty. You need only:

> A reasonable belief — based on what you've seen, heard, or been told — that a child may have been abused or neglected.

A child's disclosure alone is sufficient to trigger a report. You do not need physical evidence.

---

## 3. Reporting Process

### Step 1 — Immediate Action
If you observe, hear, or are told something that triggers reasonable suspicion, **stop and act immediately.**

Do not:
- Wait to see if it happens again
- Investigate on your own
- Ask the child leading questions
- Tell the child's parent(s) before making a report

### Step 2 — Report to Studio Director
Contact Amanda Cobb immediately:
- In person (if present)
- By phone: (949) 229-0846
- Text if cannot reach by call

### Step 3 — Studio Director Coordinates Report
Amanda will:
- Contact the appropriate reporting authority
- Support you in making the report if needed
- Document the incident in the studio's compliance records

### Step 4 — If You Cannot Reach Amanda
Make the report directly:

| Authority | Contact |
|-----------|---------|
| California Child Abuse Reporting Hotline | 1-800-540-4000 |
| San Clemente Police | 911 (emergency) or (949) 361-8200 (non-emergency) |
| Orange County Social Services | (800) 207-4464 |

### Step 5 — Document What You Observed
Write down:
- Date and time of observation
- Exactly what you saw, heard, or were told (direct quotes from child when possible)
- Names of anyone else who was present
- Any physical observations (location of injury, appearance, etc.)

Keep this documentation private. Do not share with other parents or staff not involved.

---

## 4. Protecting Yourself

**You cannot be held legally liable for a good-faith report that turns out to be unfounded.**

California law provides immunity to mandated reporters who make reports in good faith.

**You can be held legally liable if you fail to report when you had reasonable suspicion.**

Failure to report is a misdemeanor under California law, with potential criminal penalties.

---

## 5. When a Child Discloses to You

If a student tells you something concerning:

**Do:**
- Listen calmly
- Believe them
- Thank them for telling you
- Say: "You did the right thing by telling me. I'm going to make sure you're safe."
- Report immediately after the disclosure

**Don't:**
- Promise to keep it a secret
- Ask leading questions ("Did he do X to you?")
- Show visible distress or alarm that may frighten the child
- Tell them what will happen next in detail
- Discuss the disclosure with others before reporting

## 7. Additional Reporting Scenarios

### Suspected Bullying (Student-to-Student)

If you observe or are told about bullying:
1. Address the immediate classroom situation calmly
2. Do not shame the child doing the bullying in front of peers
3. Notify Amanda after class
4. Amanda will contact parents of both students
5. Document the incident

### Eating Disorder Concerns

If you observe signs consistent with disordered eating in a student:
- Do not comment on the student's body or weight
- Do not address it publicly in class
- Notify Amanda privately after class
- Amanda will determine appropriate communication with parents

This is especially important in ballet training environments. Our culture never comments on a student's weight or body composition.

### Domestic Violence Disclosure

If a student discloses witnessing domestic violence at home, this may trigger a report under California's definition of abuse.
- Report to Amanda
- Amanda will consult with reporting authority on whether a report is required

## 8. Emergency Procedures

### Medical Emergency in Class
1. Call 911 immediately for any serious injury
2. Notify Amanda or office
3. Do not move a child who may have a neck or spine injury
4. Have a staff member meet emergency services at the entrance
5. Contact parent(s) immediately — emergency contact info is in Studio Pro

### Fire Evacuation
1. Calmly direct all students to exit via marked emergency exit
2. Assembly point: parking lot, away from building
3. Do not leave children unattended
4. Account for all students against attendance sheet
5. Do not re-enter until cleared by fire department

### Lockdown / Active Threat
1. Move students away from doors and windows immediately
2. Lock classroom door if possible
3. Call 911
4. Do not open door until law enforcement clears the area
