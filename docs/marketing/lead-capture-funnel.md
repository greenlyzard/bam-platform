# Lead Capture & Conversion Funnel
## Ballet Academy and Movement

> **Goal:** Convert website visitors and social followers into enrolled students.  
> **Core principle:** Make the path from first contact to registered student as frictionless as possible — easier than e-commerce checkout.

---

## 1. Funnel Overview

```
AWARENESS
  ↓  Google search / Social / Word of mouth / Yelp
LANDING
  ↓  City SEO page / Homepage / Blog
CAPTURE
  ↓  Free trial class offer / Email capture / Chatbot
NURTURE
  ↓  Welcome email sequence (Klaviyo, 5 emails over 10 days)
CONVERSION
  ↓  Trial class → Registration
RETENTION
  ↓  Onboarding → First performance → Long-term enrollment
```

---

## 2. Primary Lead Capture Methods

### A. Free Trial Class CTA (Primary)

The highest-converting offer for dance studios is a **free trial class** — zero friction, zero commitment.

**Implementation:**
- Prominent CTA button on every city SEO page, homepage, and blog post
- Button text: "Schedule a Free Trial Class"
- Destination: Simple form (Name, Child's Name, Child's Age, Email, Phone, Preferred Day)
- After submit: Confirmation page + immediate welcome email

**Form fields (keep minimal):**
1. Parent's first name
2. Child's name
3. Child's age
4. Email address
5. Phone number
6. Preferred day of week (optional)

---

### B. Website Chatbot (Secondary)

A simple FAQ-style chatbot on the homepage and city pages captures parents who have questions but aren't ready to fill out a form.

**Trigger:** Appears after 20 seconds on page, or on scroll to 50%

**Opening message:** "Hi! Looking for ballet classes for your child? I can help you find the right fit. How old is your child?"

**Flow:**
1. Parent enters child's age
2. Bot recommends the matching class level
3. Bot asks: "Would you like to schedule a free trial?"
4. Yes → short form capture
5. No → "No problem! Here are some common questions parents ask..." → FAQ content

**Tool options:** Tidio (WordPress-compatible), Crisp, or custom-built chatbot via BAM Platform M1

---

### C. Email Capture — Content Upgrade

On high-traffic blog posts, offer a downloadable content upgrade in exchange for email.

**Examples:**
- "Download: First Ballet Class Checklist for Parents"
- "Download: Is My Child Ready for Ballet? A Parent's Guide"
- "Download: What to Bring to Ballet Class (Printable)"

These have a 3-5x higher opt-in rate than generic newsletter signups.

---

### D. Yelp — Review Optimization

Many parents find studios via Yelp. BAM should:
- Maintain 4.8+ star average
- Respond to every review (5-star and negative)
- Owner bio highlights Amanda Cobb's professional background
- Photos: updated quarterly

**Review request timing:** After performances, after a student milestone (first recital, first pointe class), after a positive check-in moment.

## 3. Welcome Email Sequence (Klaviyo)

Triggered immediately when a parent submits a trial class inquiry.

| # | Subject Line | Send Timing | Goal |
|---|-------------|-------------|------|
| 1 | "You're in! Here's what to expect at your trial class" | Immediately | Confirm, reduce anxiety, build excitement |
| 2 | "A little about Amanda and why we teach the way we do" | Day 2 | Build trust with founder story |
| 3 | "What your child will experience in class" | Day 4 | Set expectations, reduce parent nervousness |
| 4 | "What other parents say about Ballet Academy and Movement" | Day 7 | Social proof |
| 5 | "Ready to make it official? Here's how to register." | Day 10 | Conversion CTA with registration link |

**If trial class was scheduled but not attended:**
- Day 1 after missed class: "We missed you — want to reschedule?"
- Day 5: "Still thinking it over? Here's what to expect."

**If trial class was attended but not registered within 5 days:**
- Day 5: "How did [child's name]'s trial class go?"
- Day 10: "Classes are filling up for [month] — reserve your spot"

## 4. Registration Flow

The registration process must be frictionless. Target: **under 3 minutes from first click to confirmed enrollment.**

```
Step 1: Parent clicks "Register Now" or "Schedule Trial"
Step 2: Chooses child's age group
Step 3: Sees available classes for that age group
Step 4: Selects preferred class time
Step 5: Enters parent + child info (auto-fill friendly)
Step 6: Reviews class details + tuition
Step 7: Enters payment method (stored for recurring billing)
Step 8: Confirms enrollment
Step 9: Receives confirmation email with first-day details
```

**Never:**
- Require account creation before browsing classes
- Show a long form before the user has chosen their class
- Ask for information you don't need at signup

## 5. Class Placement Widget

On the website: a simple interactive tool that helps parents find the right class.

```
"How old is your child?"

[  ] Under 2  [ ] 2-3  [ ] 3-5  [ ] 5-7  [ ] 7-10  [ ] 10+

→ "Based on your child's age, we recommend: Pre-Ballet (Ages 3-5)"
  [See class schedule]  [Schedule a free trial]
```

This reduces inbound "what class should my child be in?" calls and improves conversion.
