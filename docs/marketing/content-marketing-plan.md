# Content Marketing Plan
## Ballet Academy and Movement

> **Goal:** Become the authoritative source of information for parents making ballet training decisions in South Orange County.  
> **Tone:** Warm, knowledgeable, encouraging. Never sales-y. Always parent-first.

---

## 1. Content Philosophy

Parents researching ballet classes for their child have one primary question: *Is this the right choice for my kid?*

Our content answers that question — and establishes Ballet Academy and Movement as the trusted expert before a parent ever walks through the door.

Content pillars:
1. **Educational** — what ballet actually teaches children
2. **Decision support** — helping parents choose the right program
3. **Studio insight** — why professional training matters
4. **Cultural** — the ballet world, performances, what to expect

## 6. Social Media Content Pillars

### Instagram (Primary)

**Content mix:**
- 40% — Student moments (class, rehearsal, performance)
- 25% — Educational content (tips for parents, ballet facts)
- 20% — Culture and community (behind the scenes, events)
- 15% — Promotional (enrollment open, trial class offers)

**Frequency:** 4-5 posts/week + 3-5 Stories/day

**Best-performing content types for dance studios:**
- Rehearsal clips (Reels)
- Performance highlights (Reels)
- Before/after technique progress
- Student milestone moments (first recital, pointe shoes)
- Parent testimonials (Stories)

### TikTok (Secondary)

**Content mix:**
- 50% — Behind-the-scenes rehearsal / class clips
- 30% — Educational (what does a ballet class teach your child?)
- 20% — Performance highlights

**Frequency:** 3-5 posts/week

**Hook strategy:** Every TikTok opens with a parent-relevant question:
- "Is your 3-year-old ready for ballet?"
- "What does a ballet class actually look like for a 5-year-old?"
- "How do small class sizes change everything?"

## 7. Content Production Workflow

```
1. Topic selection → based on keyword research + parent questions received
2. Brief → 1-paragraph direction for the post
3. Draft → written using BAM voice and tone guidelines
4. Review → Amanda or Derek approves
5. Publish → WordPress + Flatsome
6. Distribute → Email list (Klaviyo) + Social (Instagram, TikTok)
7. Track → Google Search Console + Analytics
```

---

## 8. Voice and Tone Guidelines

**Always:**
- Lead with the child's experience and development
- Sound like a knowledgeable, warm mentor
- Use specific, concrete language ("max 10 students" not "small classes")
- Reference Amanda Cobb's professional background when relevant
- Acknowledge parent anxieties and answer them directly

**Never:**
- Sound corporate or transactional
- Use generic phrases ("world-class," "passionate about dance")
- Over-promise results
- Compare negatively to competitor studios by name
- Use dance industry jargon without explaining it
