## Class Level Structure (BAM Curriculum)

### Pre-Ballet / Creative Movement
- **Ages:** 3–5
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Focus:** Rhythm, coordination, body awareness, imagination, joy
- **Class Length:** 45 minutes
- **Key vocabulary:** pliés, relevés, jumps, pointing toes, "ballet feet"
- **NOT called:** "Tiny Tots", "Baby Ballet", "Dance Stars" — call it Pre-Ballet
- **Marketing angle:** First exposure to classical foundations in a nurturing, playful environment

### Ballet Level 1
- **Ages:** 5–7
- **Prerequisites:** 1 year pre-ballet or audition
- **Focus:** Barre fundamentals, basic center work, introduction to positions
- **Class Length:** 45–60 minutes
- **Key vocabulary:** five positions of the feet, barre, plié, tendu, dégagé, relevé, arabesque (intro)
- **Marketing angle:** Real classical training begins here — posture, musicality, discipline

### Ballet Level 2
- **Ages:** 6–9
- **Prerequisites:** Level 1 completion or placement class
- **Focus:** Barre proficiency, center combinations, adagio basics, allegro intro
- **Class Length:** 60 minutes
- **Key vocabulary:** battement, fondu, frappé, adagio, petit allegro, port de bras
- **Marketing angle:** Where technique takes root — students begin to move like real dancers

### Ballet Level 3
- **Ages:** 8–12
- **Prerequisites:** Level 2 + teacher recommendation
- **Focus:** Full classical syllabus, pointe preparation (girls), variations
- **Class Length:** 75–90 minutes
- **Key vocabulary:** grand battement, développé, arabesque, attitude, pirouette prep, pas de deux (intro)
- **Marketing angle:** Serious training for dedicated students — where potential becomes artistry

### Pointe Preparation / Pre-Pointe
- **Ages:** 10+ (bone structure assessment required)
- **Prerequisites:** Level 3 + teacher assessment of foot/ankle strength
- **Focus:** Foot and ankle strengthening, alignment, Thera-band work
- **Note:** Never rush students to pointe. Bone maturity matters more than eagerness.
- **Marketing angle:** Pointe is earned, not given — we take the health of every student seriously

### Pointe
- **Ages:** 11+ (with teacher clearance)
- **Prerequisites:** Pointe prep completion + teacher clearance
- **Focus:** Pointe work at barre, center pointe combinations, variations on pointe
- **Class Length:** 30–45 min add-on to regular Level 3+ class

## Supplemental Programs

### Jazz
- **Ages:** 7–12
- **Style:** Theatrical jazz rooted in classical technique
- **NOT:** Commercial hip-hop or competition jazz
- **Key vocabulary:** jazz walks, turns, kicks, isolations, stylized combinations

### Contemporary
- **Ages:** 9–12
- **Style:** Grounded in classical ballet, incorporating floor work and modern elements
- **Key vocabulary:** release technique, contraction, spiral, floorwork, lyrical phrase

### Musical Theatre
- **Ages:** 6–12
- **Style:** Dance and performance skills for stage — acting through movement
- **Key vocabulary:** character work, projection, storytelling, ensemble

## Skill Taxonomy (for Badge System + LMS)

### Foundational Skills (Pre-Ballet → Level 1)
- Five positions of the feet
- Five positions of the arms
- Plié (demi and grand)
- Relevé
- Pointing the foot (tendu)
- Arabesque (simple)
- Balance on one foot
- Rhythmic awareness (count to 8)
- Stage directions (downstage, upstage, wings)

### Developing Skills (Level 1 → Level 2)
- Dégagé
- Battement
- Fondu
- Frappé
- Port de bras sequences
- Adagio combinations
- Petit allegro (small jumps)
- Épaulement (shoulder/head positioning)
- Coordination of arms + legs

### Advancing Skills (Level 2 → Level 3)
- Grand battement
- Développé
- Attitude (front, side, back)
- Pirouette preparation
- Pirouette (single)
- Pas de bourrée
- Chassé sequences
- Musicality — phrasing, dynamics
- Performance quality

### Advanced Skills (Level 3 + Pointe Prep)
- Pirouette (double)
- Grand allegro combinations
- Variation learning
- Pointe preparation assessment
- Full body alignment mastery
- Artistic interpretation

## Parent Education Reference

### Common Parent Questions + Correct Answers

**"Is my 3-year-old too young to start?"**
3 is the ideal starting age for Pre-Ballet. The focus is entirely developmentally appropriate — rhythm, body awareness, imagination. There's no pressure to perform technique correctly. It's about building a foundation and a love for movement.

**"Will ballet make my daughter's feet turn out permanently?"**
Proper ballet training develops turnout from the hips — not forced turnout from the feet. Our instructors are trained in classical pedagogy that prioritizes healthy alignment. We never force or rush turnout development.

**"Do I need to buy a lot of equipment?"**
For Pre-Ballet and early levels: leotard, tights, and ballet slippers. That's it. No pointe shoes until much later, and never without instructor clearance.

**"My child wants to be on a competition team."**
BAM is a classical ballet training studio, not a competition dance studio. We focus on strong classical foundations. Our students who pursue performance opportunities do so through intensives, auditions, and our own productions like The Nutcracker.

**"How quickly will my child progress?"**
Progress in classical ballet is measured in years, not weeks. What you'll notice first: posture, body awareness, musicality, and confidence. Technical milestones come later and are deeply individual.

## Intensives & Summer Programs (Proof Points)

BAM students have been accepted to:
- **Royal Ballet School** intensive (London — most prestigious in the world)
- **American Ballet Theatre** (ABT) summer intensive (NYC)
- **Stuttgart Ballet** intensive (Germany)

These are not regional programs. These are the most competitive ballet intensives in the world.
Use these in marketing carefully but prominently — they are the strongest proof of training quality.
