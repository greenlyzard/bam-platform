# Brand Guidelines
## Ballet Academy and Movement

> **Aesthetic:** Minimal luxury ballet studio. Feminine, modern, refined.  
> **Positioning:** "Real ballet training in a nurturing environment."  
> **Do not abbreviate as "BAM" in public-facing content** — trademark conflict.

---

## 1. Brand Personality

Ballet Academy and Movement is the studio where serious ballet meets genuine warmth.

| Attribute | What This Means |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Refined** | Every design choice communicates quality, intention, and taste |
| **Warm** | Never cold or intimidating — always welcoming |
| **Knowledgeable** | The expertise of a professional ballet career, communicated accessibly |
| **Encouraging** | Celebrates progress; never shame-based |
| **Nurturing** | Children are safe, seen, and celebrated here |

**The brand is not:**
- Cutesy or cartoonish
- Generic fitness studio / gym aesthetic
- Competition-factory energy (trophies, rankings, intensity)
- Corporate or clinical
- Loud or aggressive

## 3. Typography

### Heading Font — Cormorant Garamond
- **Use for:** Page titles, section headers, display text, performance program titles
- **Weight:** Light (300) for elegant display; Regular (400) for readability
- **Style:** Serif, classical, editorial
- **Source:** Google Fonts (free)

### Body / UI Font — Inter or DM Sans
- **Use for:** Body copy, navigation, buttons, form labels, captions
- **Weight:** Regular (400) for body; Medium (500) for emphasis; Semibold (600) for UI labels
- **Style:** Clean sans-serif, high readability at small sizes
- **Source:** Google Fonts (free)

### Hierarchy Example

```
H1 — Cormorant Garamond Light, 48-64px, Lavender or Charcoal
H2 — Cormorant Garamond Regular, 32-40px, Charcoal
H3 — Cormorant Garamond Regular, 24-28px, Charcoal
Body — Inter Regular, 16-18px, Charcoal
Caption — Inter Regular, 14px, Medium Gray
Button — Inter Semibold, 14-16px, White on Lavender
```

## 4. Logo Usage

### Primary Logo
- Full name: "Ballet Academy and Movement"
- Use on: All official communications, website header, printed materials
- Minimum size: Never smaller than 120px wide in digital; 1.5" in print

### Logo Clearspace
- Maintain minimum clearspace equal to the height of the "B" in Ballet around all sides

### Color Versions
| Version | When to Use |
|---------|------------|
| Lavender on Cream | Primary — most materials |
| White on Lavender | Dark backgrounds, header bars |
| Charcoal on White/Cream | Monochrome print contexts |
| Gold on Black | Competition Company materials |

### What Not to Do With the Logo
- Do not stretch or distort
- Do not apply drop shadows or gradients
- Do not place on busy photographic backgrounds without overlay
- Do not abbreviate to "BAM" in the logo or any public-facing context

## 5. Iconography Style

Use **thin-stroke line icons** that feel elegant and hand-drawn, not chunky or cartoonish.

### Preferred Icon Subjects (ballet-specific)
- Pointe shoe
- Ballet barre
- Tutu silhouette
- Stage curtain
- Music staff / note
- Crown / tiara (for achievements)
- Ribbon or laurel (for awards)

### Icon Style Rules
- Stroke weight: 1-1.5px
- Rounded line caps preferred
- No fill (outline only) for UI contexts
- Filled version (lavender or gold) for achievement badges only
- Never use generic business SaaS icons (gear, bar chart, briefcase) in student-facing materials

## 6. Photography Style

### What to Show
- Students in class — candid moments of joy, concentration, correction
- Performances — stage lighting, costumed students, curtain calls
- Studio interior — clean, elegant, light-filled
- Amanda Cobb teaching — shows the professional environment
- Student milestones — first pointe shoes, first Nutcracker

### Photography Guidelines
- Light and airy — no dark, moody editorial style
- Natural or warm artificial light
- Subjects should look engaged, joyful, or focused (not posed awkwardly)
- Avoid photos where studio logo or branding is unclear or absent
- Always have current photo release signed before using any student photos publicly

### What to Avoid
- Stock photography that looks generic
- Over-edited filters (especially dark, desaturated, or heavy vignettes)
- Competition-style photos with heavy makeup on young children
- Photos where the background is cluttered or distracting

## 7. Content Tone & Voice

### Always
- Lead with the child's experience and development
- Sound like a knowledgeable, warm mentor
- Use specific, concrete language: "max 10 students" not "small classes"
- Reference Amanda's professional background when relevant
- Acknowledge parent questions and answer them directly

### Never
- Use generic phrases: "world-class," "passionate about dance," "your child's journey"
- Sound corporate or transactional
- Over-promise results or outcomes
- Compare negatively to competitor studios by name
- Use dance industry jargon without explaining it
- Discuss pricing in a way that sounds cheap or discount-focused

### Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Website / landing pages | Warm, credentialed, clear |
| Instagram | Celebratory, behind-the-scenes, encouraging |
| TikTok | Educational, approachable, slightly less formal |
| Email to parents | Direct, warm, professional |
| Performance programs | Elevated, editorial, celebratory |
| Job postings | Professional, culture-focused, high-standards |
